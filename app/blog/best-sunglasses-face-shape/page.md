## How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before diving into recommendations, let's identify your face shape. Here's a quick method:

1. **Pull your hair back** and look straight into a mirror
2. **Trace the outline** of your face on the mirror with a washable marker or lipstick
3. **Compare the shape** to the descriptions below

Alternatively, measure these four areas:
- **Forehead**: Across the widest part
- **Cheekbones**: From the outer corner of each eye
- **Jawline**: From the widest point to your chin
- **Face length**: From hairline to chin

## 1. Oval Face Shape

### Characteristics
- Balanced proportions
- Slightly wider cheekbones
- Gentle jawline curve
- Face length > width

**Celebrity examples**: Rihanna, Jessica Alba, Robert Pattinson

### ✅ Best Sunglasses Styles

| Style | Why It Works | Popular Models |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| Wayfarer | Maintains natural balance | Classic Black, Tortoise |
| Aviator | Complements proportions | Gold Frame, Silver Mirror |
| Rectangle | Adds definition | Modern Rectangle |
| Cat Eye | Adds playful angles | Vintage Cat Eye |

### ❌ Styles to Avoid
- Oversized frames (can overwhelm features)
- Frames wider than your cheekbones

Oval faces are versatile—experiment with bold colors and unique shapes!

## FAQ: Sunglasses & Face Shapes

### Q: Can I wear any sunglasses style if I like it?
**A:** Absolutely! These are guidelines, not rules. Confidence is the best accessory. However, understanding what flatters your features helps you make informed choices.

### Q: How important is face shape when buying sunglasses?
**A:** Very important for both aesthetics and comfort. Properly fitted frames feel better and look more polished. For retail buyers, stocking diverse styles ensures you serve all customers.

### Q: What if my face doesn't fit one category?
**A:** Most people are a mix of two shapes. Identify your dominant characteristics and choose frames that address those features.

### Q: Do expensive sunglasses fit better?
**A:** Not necessarily. Price doesn't guarantee fit. Focus on frame measurements (lens width, bridge width, temple length) rather than brand name.

### Q: How do I measure sunglasses size?
**A:** Look for three numbers on the temple: Lens Width - Bridge Width - Temple Length (e.g., 52-18-140). These should match your face measurements.
